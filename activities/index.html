Things to do in Southwest Colorado

Outdoor Activities

Fun for Everyone in the Mountains

Hiking and Camping

The San Juan mountains offer some amazing hiking and camping opportunities for everything from a quick walk to multi-day backpacking trails.    If you're interested in learning more, please let us know or check out some of the options listed here.

Tours and Parks

If you'd like to get to get outside with a little less walking, there are some great organized options.

  • The Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad is a historic coal-fired train that makes several trips daily between Durango and Silverton on a route along the beautiful Animas river.
  • Several vendors also offer jeep tours in the mountains around Telluride, Silverton, and Ouray--an area famous for its 4x4 trails.  Some companies offering tours include Mild To Wild and San Juan Backcountry.  If you're feeling adventurous, you can also rent your own.

Mesa Verde and The Black Canyon of the Gunnison are nearby national parks with beautiful scenery well-developed access and facilities.

Adrenaline

Looking for something more exciting?  There are adventure sport options for every taste, including:

  • Mountain biking
  • Whitewater rafting and kayaking
  • Zipline tours
  • Glider tours
  • Rock climbing

Art and Culture

Museums and History

Explore the natural and human history of Southwest Colorado:

  • Mesa Verde National Park
  • D&SNGRR Railroad Museum and Yard Tours
  • Tours of the Old Hundred Mine (Silverton), Bachelor of Syracuse Mine (Ouray), or Mayflower gold mill (Silverton)
  • Museums in Durango, Silverton, and Telluride

Treat yo' Self

Food, Spa, and Shopping

Looking for something more civilized?  There's also plenty of spas, shopping, and hot springs to let you relax in style.  Durango and Silverton (to a lesser degree) offer many options for shopping, eating, and relaxation.  A bit further afield, Telluride has a selection of high end spas and resturaunts.
